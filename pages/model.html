Model Overview

Eutrophication Risk Prediction

ChitoNet uses a Gradient Boosting machine learning model to predict eutrophication risk based on water quality parameters. The model analyzes real-time sensor data from pH, temperature, turbidity, and chlorophyll-a levels to provide early warning of algal bloom conditions.

Unlike traditional monitoring approaches that detect problems after they occur, our ML model identifies risk patterns days or weeks before visible algal blooms appear, enabling proactive intervention and prevention.

The model was trained on a comprehensive dataset of 268,361 samples combining real-world measurements and synthetically generated data to ensure robust generalization across diverse water conditions.
